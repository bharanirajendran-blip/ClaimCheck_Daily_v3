Source: FactCheck.org

Confidence: 80%

Evidence sources (2): factcheck.orgepa.gov

The claim that glyphosate weedkiller causes cancer is contested. Regulatory agencies like the EPA have found glyphosate unlikely to be carcinogenic at approved use levels, while the WHO's IARC classified it as 'probably carcinogenic' based on animal studies and limited human evidence. The scientific community remains divided, with some studies showing associations with cancer in occupational settings but not in typical consumer exposure.

Key Evidence
  • The EPA's official position states that glyphosate is unlikely to be a human carcinogen when used according to its label (EPA.gov).
  • The WHO's IARC classified glyphosate as 'probably carcinogenic to humans' based on animal studies and limited human evidence (FactCheck.org).
  • A large NIH study did not find an association between glyphosate and non-Hodgkin lymphoma or other cancers in agricultural workers (FactCheck.org).
  • There is little evidence to suggest glyphosate causes cancer in the trace amounts present in food (FactCheck.org).
