The claim that vaccines cause autism is conclusively false. It originated from a fraudulent study by Andrew Wakefield, which has been retracted, and extensive scientific research has shown no causal link between vaccines and autism. Major health organizations worldwide confirm this conclusion.

Key Evidence
  • The original Wakefield study was retracted by The Lancet in 2010 due to fraud and ethical violations.
  • Multiple large-scale studies involving millions of children have found no link between vaccines and autism.
  • The WHO confirms that vaccines are safe and effective, undergoing rigorous safety testing.
  • Major health authorities such as the CDC and WHO state there is no causal link between vaccines and autism.
