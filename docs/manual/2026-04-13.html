"Apollo 11 landed on the Moon in 1969."

Source: Manual Input

Confidence: 100%

The claim that Apollo 11 landed on the Moon in 1969 is fully verified. Both NASA and Britannica confirm that Apollo 11's lunar module Eagle landed on the Moon on July 20, 1969, with Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin becoming the first humans to walk on the lunar surface.

Key Evidence
  • Apollo 11, the first space mission to put people on the Moon, was launched on July 16, 1969. Almost every major aspect of the flight of Apollo 11 was witnessed via television by hundreds of millions of people in nearly every part of the globe, until splashdown in the Pacific Ocean on July 24. (Britannica)
  • Apollo 11’s mission objective was to land astronauts on the Moon and return them safely back to Earth. It succeeded in its objective by landing Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin on the Moon on July 20, 1969. (Britannica)
  • The lunar module Eagle of the Apollo 11 mission landed on the Moon on July 20, 1969. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first people to land on the Moon and walk on the lunar surface. (Britannica)
  • NASA maintains an official dedicated mission page for Apollo 11 at nasa.gov/mission/apollo-11/ (NASA)
