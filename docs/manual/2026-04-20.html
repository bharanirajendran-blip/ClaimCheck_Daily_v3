The claim that 'glyphosate causes cancer' is contested. The IARC (WHO) classified glyphosate as 'probably carcinogenic to humans' based on limited human evidence and sufficient animal evidence. However, the EPA and EFSA have concluded it is unlikely to be carcinogenic at typical exposure levels. The evidence indicates a potential cancer hazard but does not definitively establish causation in real-world exposures. The claim lacks nuance regarding dose, exposure context, and the distinction between hazard and risk.
