"Politicians Say Glyphosate Weedkiller Causes Cancer But Evidence Not Clear-Cut"

Source: FactCheck.org

Confidence: 85%

Evidence sources (2): factcheck.orgepa.gov

The claim that glyphosate causes cancer is not clear-cut. While some evidence links glyphosate to cancer, particularly non-Hodgkin lymphoma in agricultural workers, findings are inconsistent. Regulatory agencies, including the EPA, have generally concluded that glyphosate is unlikely to be carcinogenic at typical exposure levels. Politicians' definitive claims that glyphosate 'causes cancer' oversimplify the nuanced scientific evidence.

Key Evidence
  • The WHO's International Agency for Research on Cancer classified glyphosate as 'probably carcinogenic to humans' based on animal studies and limited human evidence.
  • A large NIH study found no association between glyphosate and non-Hodgkin lymphoma or other cancers in agricultural workers.
  • The EPA's 2020 assessment stated glyphosate is unlikely to be a human carcinogen, but this was vacated by the Ninth Circuit Court, leaving the regulatory status in flux.
  • There is little evidence to suggest glyphosate causes cancer in the trace amounts found in food, distinguishing it from occupational exposure.

Source: Science Daily — Top Science

The claim that a new toothpaste stops gum disease without killing good bacteria is mostly true. The Fraunhofer Institute developed a compound that inhibits harmful bacteria like Porphyromonas gingivalis without affecting beneficial bacteria, which helps maintain oral microbiome balance. However, the claim simplifies the product's role in preventing rather than curing gum disease.

Key Evidence
  • Fraunhofer IZI's research confirms the development of a compound that selectively blocks harmful pathogens such as Porphyromonas gingivalis without affecting other bacteria.
  • The compound inhibits bacterial growth rather than killing bacteria, allowing beneficial microbes to thrive and restore balance.
  • Stephan Schilling, Head of Fraunhofer IZI branch, explains the mechanism as inhibiting the growth of harmful bacteria, which allows beneficial bacteria to occupy ecological niches.
  • The toothpaste product is confirmed to be on the market, developed by PerioTrap Pharmaceuticals GmbH.
