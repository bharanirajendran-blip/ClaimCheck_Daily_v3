"Vaccines cause autism."

Source: Manual Input

Confidence: 95%

Evidence sources (3): who.intnhs.ukunknown source

There is no scientific evidence supporting the claim that vaccines cause autism. Vaccines are designed to protect against diseases without causing the diseases themselves or related complications.

Key Evidence
  • Vaccines contain only killed or weakened forms of germs like viruses or bacteria, and they do not cause the disease or put you at risk of its complications. (Source: WHO)
  • The MMR vaccine helps protect against measles, mumps, and rubella, and there is no evidence linking it to autism. (Source: NHS)
